The Role of an Immigration Attorney and 12 Questions to Ask When Looking for One


he role of an immigration attorney is unique when compared with other types of attorneys. Immigration attorneys assist people who are having difficulty understanding and dealing with all current U.S. immigration requirements.
The immigration attorney is an advisor/counselor to citizens of foreign countries and immigrants who are interacting with U.S. immigration authorities. They provide advice and guidance for several matters that include but are not limited to; visa applications, deportation issues, citizenship and naturalization, green and employment for non-citizens.
Although Immigration Attorneys do make appearances before immigration judges, in cases such as deportation hearings, typically they spend far less time in court than other attorneys. The majority of their time is spent acting as mediator between their clients and immigration authorities such as USCIS, U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services.
When looking for an Immigration attorney here are some tips to help you.
  1. How long have you been practicing law?
  2. In what state(s) are you licensed to practice law?
  3. What percentage of your clients are immigration cases?
  4. What is your percentage of favorable outcomes?
  5. Are you a member of the American Immigration Lawyers Association?
  6. How often do you handle immigration matters like my case?
  7. What is the range of possible outcomes in a case like mine from best to worst?
  8. Do you charge an hourly rate of a flat fee?
  9. When will payment be due?
  10. Do you work as a team, who else in the office will work on your case?
  11. What is the best way to communicate with you? Phone, email, Text?
  12. What is their policy for returning calls and/or e-mails?
